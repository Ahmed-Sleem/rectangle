# General Work Management (Partial Overlap)

These tools win knowledge-work PM. Construction firms use them when they lack budget for Procore or for **office** teams only.

## monday.com
- Highly configurable boards, dashboards, automations  
- monday AI assistants  
- Construction templates exist but not deep commercial construction  
- Great IA lesson: **building blocks** (boards, items, columns) users reshape  

## ClickUp
- Extreme feature density: docs, goals, whiteboards, AI (ClickUp Brain)  
- Risk: cluttered UX  
- Hierarchy: Workspace → Space → Folder → List → Task  

## Asana
- Clean tasks/projects, workload, portfolios (higher tiers)  
- Asana Intelligence  
- Excellent clarity UX; weak construction objects  

## Smartsheet
- Spreadsheet paradigm + forms + dashboards + Control Center  
- Popular with ops/PMO hybrid users  
- AI automation  
- Good bridge for Excel-native construction admins  

## Wrike
- Flexible workflows, Gantt, proofing  
- Sometimes used in construction offices  

## Zoho Projects
- Affordable; **Arabic support** cited in GCC roundups  
- Good SMB international option  

## Linear (reference only)
- Opinionated speed UX for software — gold standard for keyboard/command palette; not construction  

## Takeaways for Tornix-like GUI
1. Command palette (ClickUp/Linear/Plane)  
2. Multiple views on same object (list/board/gantt/calendar/spreadsheet)  
3. Progressive complexity (Asana clarity vs ClickUp kitchen sink — pick a side)  
4. Automation builder as retention hook  
5. Don’t pretend generic work management beats RFI/drawing workflows without custom objects
