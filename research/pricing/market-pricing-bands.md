# Market pricing bands — products similar to an AI construction / PMO platform

**Important:** Tornix itself does **not** publish list pricing (custom / contact sales).  
Figures below are **industry ranges** from public third-party reports and vendor pages (2025–2026). Always re-quote before budgeting.

## Absolute floor → ceiling

| Band | Rough range | Examples of what sits there |
|------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Free / open | **$0** | Fieldwire free tier, OpenProject Community self-host, free PM tiers |
| SMB construction all-in-one | **~$50–$900 / month** | Contractor Foreman ~$49–$332/mo; Buildertrend ~$299–$900+/mo |
| Per-seat field / design-build | **~$15–$140 / user / month** | Fieldwire ~$39–$89/user; Autodesk Build / Docs seat SKUs |
| Mid full platform | **~$5k–$60k / year** | Mid Buildertrend annual; mid Procore / multi-seat ACC |
| Enterprise construction OS | **~$15k–$150k+ / year** subscription | Procore ACV model; large ACC / Oracle stacks |
| Year-1 enterprise + services | **~$70k–$300k+** | Subscription + implementation + training + integrations |
| AI specialists (add-on) | **~$50k–$150k+/year** or per-project | ALICE-class schedule AI; Buildots often per area tracked |

## Tornix-like “full AI PMO” competitive window

Most realistic **peer band** for a bilingual AI construction/PMO product:

| | Annual |
|--|--------|
| **Low** | ~$3,000 – $12,000 |
| **Mid** | ~$12,000 – $60,000 |
| **High** | ~$60,000 – $200,000+ |

## Model patterns

| Model | Used by |
|-------|---------|
| Annual construction volume (ACV) | Procore |
| Per user / month | Fieldwire, many ACC seats, generic PM |
| Flat monthly unlimited users | Some SMB construction tools |
| Custom enterprise quote | Oracle, large ACC, AI specialists, Tornix |
| Self-host free + paid enterprise | OpenProject, Plane-class OSS |

## Caveats

- Implementation can add **30–100%+** on year one.  
- AI usage, storage, and support tiers may be extra.  
- MENA deals may be packaged in SAR/AED with local partners.  
- Sources disagree; treat as **order-of-magnitude**, not invoices.
